# Carbon Cut Stitch Brief

Use this brief to generate homepage directions for Carbon Cut.

## Core Direction

Carbon Cut should not look like generic startup SaaS, climate consulting software, or polished enterprise climate-tech.

It should feel:

- credible
- climate-serious
- slightly technical
- light
- airy
- distinctive
- memorable
- less corporate than competitors

It should not feel:

- generic
- consultancy-like
- enterprise-polished
- like a climate-tech template
- emotionally flat

## Design Feel

The homepage should feel:

- authored
- specific
- more alive than competitor websites
- serious without being corporate
- distinctive without becoming chaotic
- lighter and fresher than enterprise climate-tech

Do not overcorrect toward:

- safe professionalism
- overly clean enterprise layouts
- polished generic product marketing

## Color and Typography

Use the approved Carbon Cut theme:

- dark teal / green-teal primary text
- vivid orange highlight accent
- pale cool light backgrounds
- soft white surfaces
- typography that feels strong and readable without drifting into prestige-editorial styling

Do not make the interface feel:

- beige editorial
- dark and moody
- premium enterprise
- architectural consultancy
- cold in a corporate way

## Layout

Use one homepage concept only.

Desktop and mobile must be responsive adaptations of the same concept, not separate creative directions.

The page should feel like a guided argument with visual identity, not a collection of generic marketing sections.

It should also stay emotionally closer to the current Carbon Cut feeling than to later dark green / infrastructure-style experiments.

## Product Truth

Carbon Cut is currently:

- a testing-stage climate product
- a guided personal carbon-footprint assessment
- focused on transport and home energy inputs
- returning a structured result with category-oriented visuals and recommendations

Do not claim or imply:

- businesses, restaurants, or municipalities as current supported product flows
- enterprise workflows
- teams or workspaces
- integrations or APIs
- real-time tracking
- audit-ready reporting
- exports
- pricing or live subscriptions unless explicitly confirmed

## Homepage Goal

The homepage must:

- establish Carbon Cut as credible and climate-serious
- explain the current product in plain language
- present Carbon Cut as a guided personal carbon assessment
- make the next step clear

The homepage must not:

- drift into generic SaaS
- overpromise product maturity
- invent unsupported product capabilities

## Homepage Structure

Use this section order:

1. Hero
2. What Carbon Cut Does
3. How It Works
4. Results and Recommendations
5. Product Status and Trust
6. Final CTA

## Content Source

Use `homepage-implementation.md` as the source for homepage copy and hierarchy.

Do not rewrite the meaning.

You may shorten for layout, but do not invent new claims or sections.

## Warning Signs To Avoid

Do not produce:

- fake enterprise navigation
- pricing pages or demo CTAs by default
- dashboard-heavy hero visuals
- generic testimonial sections
- feature-card overload
- climate consulting aesthetics
- pseudo-authoritative trust badges
- competitor-like climate-tech polish
- architectural infrastructure aesthetics
- premium editorial enterprise mood
- dark forest-green prestige branding

## Final Test

The output should feel like Carbon Cut, not just like a well-designed climate-tech website.
