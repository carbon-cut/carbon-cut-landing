# Carbon Cut Product Truth Source

This file defines the current product truth for Carbon Cut.

Use it as the reference before writing homepage copy, product copy, sales copy, or UI messaging.

If a marketing claim conflicts with this file, this file wins.

Carbon Cut is still in testing.

Any copy about the product should stay conservative and avoid implying production maturity.

## What Carbon Cut Currently Does

Carbon Cut currently provides these implemented product behaviors:

- It lets a signed-in user complete a guided carbon questionnaire.
- It collects structured user inputs about transport and home energy.
- It submits those answers to a backend carbon-footprint calculation endpoint.
- It returns the user to a results experience after form submission.
- It displays a headline carbon-footprint result in tons of CO2.
- It displays a result interface with category-oriented visuals.
- It supports account creation and sign-in.
- It has a homepage that directs users into the form flow.

Implementation notes:

- The current active frontend flow is centered on personal footprint estimation.
- The current active frontend scope should be treated as `transport` and `energie` only.
- Some result UI values are still presentation-layer values rather than fully derived reporting.
- The product should be described as a testing-stage product, not a production-mature product.

## What Carbon Cut Does Not Do

Carbon Cut does not currently provide these capabilities in a shipped, reliable way:

- A true business carbon accounting workflow.
- A restaurant-specific operational workflow.
- A municipality-specific workflow.
- Team collaboration or multi-user workspaces.
- Continuous emissions tracking over time.
- Audit-ready or compliance-ready reporting.
- Report exports.
- Live subscriptions or confirmed billing flows.
- Carbon offset purchasing or verified compensation flows.
- Expert consulting services inside the product.
- Enterprise integrations, API access, or external system ingestion.
- Rigorous benchmarking against local, sector, or global peer groups.

## What Is Planned But Not Implemented

These directions exist in current design or messaging, but should not be treated as live product truth:

- Support for audiences beyond individuals, especially restaurant owners and municipalities.
- A homepage narrative that presents Carbon Cut as one platform for several audience types.
- More complete results reporting and stronger evidence/proof sections.
- More category coverage such as waste and vacation inputs in the active form flow.
- Restoration or expansion of additional categories beyond transport and energy.
- More advanced recommendations and reduction planning.
- Real pricing and plan positioning tied to a purchasable offer.
- Stronger trust sections based on validated methodology or real outcomes.

Planned means it is a direction.

Planned does not mean it is implemented.

## What Claims Are Safe On The Homepage

These claims are currently safe:

- Carbon Cut helps users estimate their carbon footprint through a guided questionnaire.
- Carbon Cut helps users understand major emissions areas such as transport and energy.
- Carbon Cut turns user inputs into a structured footprint result.
- Carbon Cut guides users step by step through the assessment flow.
- Carbon Cut is built for people who want to understand and start reducing their footprint.
- Carbon Cut presents results in a clear interface.
- Carbon Cut includes recommendations or guidance after the calculation flow.
- Carbon Cut is a testing-stage climate product focused on practical carbon awareness and action.

Preferred framing:

- "Measure and understand your footprint."
- "Get a guided carbon assessment."
- "See where your emissions come from."
- "Start with a personal carbon estimate and practical next steps."

## What Claims Are Forbidden On The Homepage

Do not claim any of the following unless the product is actually implemented and verified:

- That Carbon Cut already serves businesses, restaurants, or municipalities in production.
- That Carbon Cut is a full carbon management platform.
- That Carbon Cut delivers audit-ready reporting.
- That Carbon Cut offers real-time tracking or continuous monitoring.
- That Carbon Cut supports teams, departments, or organization-wide collaboration.
- That Carbon Cut includes verified offsetting or compensation.
- That Carbon Cut has enterprise integrations or API access.
- That Carbon Cut has live paid plans or purchasable subscriptions unless billing is confirmed.
- That Carbon Cut provides expert consulting.
- That Carbon Cut offers rigorous local or global benchmark comparisons.
- That Carbon Cut can calculate a company's footprint through the current implemented flow.
- That Carbon Cut has validated emissions-reduction outcomes unless evidence exists.

Terms to avoid unless backed by implementation:

- platform
- enterprise
- real-time
- reporting
- compliance
- audit-ready
- team workspace
- business carbon management
- municipality
- compensation
- offsetting

## Target Users

Current primary target user:

- An individual user who wants a guided way to estimate and understand their personal carbon footprint.

Current secondary target user:

- A climate-aware person looking for practical first steps to understand personal emissions.

Planned but not current target users:

- Restaurant owners.
- Businesses or operational teams.
- Municipalities or public-sector actors.

Homepage rule:

- The homepage may mention future audience expansion only as future direction.
- The homepage must not present those audiences as fully supported current users.
